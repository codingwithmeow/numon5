In the Agriculture

Molecular analysis of liquids, solids, and all raw materials and products within the food value chain is conducted using infrared photonic spectroscopy. This technology enables the analysis of components that were previously examined using large machines with high energy expenditure, now requiring much less energy.

Additionally, it allows for the analysis not only of the outer surfaces but also the interiors of fruits, vegetables, or raw materials. Through this method, products are tracked from their inclusion in the production chain, starting from the growing process underground to the collection steps in the field.
